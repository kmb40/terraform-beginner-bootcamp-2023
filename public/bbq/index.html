The history of smoking meat can be traced back to the Paleolithic era, when early humans discovered that smoking meat could help to preserve it and make it more palatable. Smoke contains compounds that have antimicrobial and antioxidant properties, which can help to slow down the growth of bacteria and other microorganisms. Smoking also changes the flavor and texture of meat, giving it a unique and delicious taste.
